🏗️ Master Blueprint: The Management &
Settings Hub
I. Management (The Agency Brain)
Goal: High-level oversight and operational control.

   ●​ Branch/Office Management: If you expand to multiple locations (e.g., CDMX and
      Cancún), manage separate addresses, tax IDs (RFCs), and local bank accounts.
   ●​ Supplier Master List: A global view of all partners with a "Kimi Reliability Score"
      (AI-calculated based on past booking issues vs. successful trips).
   ●​ Document Templates: Centralized storage for:
          ○​ Terms & Conditions: (Mexican Consumer Protection / PROFECO compliant).
          ○​ Privacy Notices: (LFPDPPP compliant).
          ○​ Service Agreements: Branded with your logo.



II. Marketing (Automation & AI Campaigns)
Goal: Turn "One-Time Travelers" into "Lifetime Clients" without manual effort.

1. AI-Driven Segmentation
   ●​ Traditional Segments: [Honeymooners, Luxury, Budget, Families, Adventure].
   ●​ Kimi "Smart" Segments: AI analyzes behavior to create groups like: "Clients who travel
      every November" or "Users who opened the Japan proposal but didn't book."

2. Automation Workflows (The "Set & Forget")
   ●​ The "Welcome Home" Loop:
         ○​ T+1 Day: WhatsApp: "Welcome back! How was the flight?"
         ○​ T+7 Days: Email: "We'd love your feedback (and a Google Review)."
         ○​ T+14 Days: Email: "Based on your trip to Paris, here are 3 similar 'Culture' trips
             for next year."
   ●​ Abandoned Proposal Recovery: If a client hasn't clicked their "Magic Link" in 48 hours,
      Kimi drafts a gentle follow-up: "Hi Juan, I saw you haven't had a chance to look at the
      Oaxaca plan. Prices for the hotel we picked are rising—should we lock it in?"

3. Campaign Builder
   ●​ Blast WhatsApp/Email: Send seasonal promos (e.g., "Buen Fin" or "Black Friday") to
      specific segments.
   ●​ AI Content Generator: Kimi writes the subject lines and body text to ensure high open
      rates.
III. Settings (Team Roles & Permissions)
Goal: Security and accountability.

1. Team Roles (RBAC - Role-Based Access Control)
 Role              Access Level



 Owner             Full access to everything (Financials, Deleting Records, Settings).



 Manager           View all team leads, approve proposals, view agency-wide reports.



 Agent             View/Edit only their assigned Leads and Clients. No access to "Agency
                   Profit" stats.



 Accountant        Access to Financials and Commissions only. Read-only on Itineraries.



 External DMC      (Optional) Restricted view for local partners to see only the clients they are
                   hosting.



2. Team Tracking
   ●​ Lead Rotation: Define how new leads are assigned (Round Robin, by Specialty, or
      "First to Claim").
   ●​ Activity Logs: See exactly who changed a price or edited a passport number.



IV. Integrations (The Tech Stack)
Goal: Connectivity with the tools you already use.

   ●​ Communication: WhatsApp Business API, SendGrid (Email), Twilio (SMS).
   ●​ Financials: QuickBooks / Xero (Accounting) and Stripe / Openpay (Mexico-friendly
      payments).
   ●​ Travel Tools: GDS (Amadeus/Sabre), Bedsonline API, or Travefy.
   ●​ Productivity: Google Workspace (Calendar/Drive), Slack (Internal alerts).
   ●​ Kimi k2.5 API: The central logic engine connecting all the above.



V. Developer Logic: The "AI Maintenance" Tab
Your developer should add a "Kimi Health" section in Settings where you can:

   1.​ Adjust the "Voice": (e.g., "Switch tone from 'Formal' to 'Friendly/Adventurous'").
   2.​ Prompt Training: Upload your agency's best past itineraries so Kimi "learns" exactly
       how you like to plan.
   3.​ Audit AI Usage: Track how many tokens/requests Kimi is using to manage costs.

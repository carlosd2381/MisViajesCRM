🏗️ Master Blueprint: Financials &
Commission Engine
I. Global Logic & Database Architecture
   ●​ Dual-Currency Ledger: Every transaction must store the Original Currency (e.g., USD
      for a cruise) and the Local Currency (MXN for the client invoice) using the exchange
      rate at the time of the transaction.
   ●​ The "Net-Gross-Profit" Triangle:
          ○​ Net: What the supplier charges the agency.
          ○​ Gross: What the client pays.
          ○​ Commission: Gross - Net (or a fixed % of Gross).
   ●​ Tax Compliance (Mexico): Native support for CFDI 4.0. The system must distinguish
      between "Intermediate Services" (commission-based) and "Direct Sales" (service fees)
      for SAT reporting.



II. Module 1: Commissions (Tracker & Reconciliation)
Goal: Ensure the agency actually receives the money it's owed.

1. The Commission Tracker (Pending vs. Received)
   ●​ Fields: Supplier_ID, Expected_Amount, Due_Date (Auto-set to X days after client
      travel), Status (Unclaimed, Claimed, Paid, Disputed).
   ●​ AI Feature (Kimi "The Auditor"): Kimi scans all "Completed" trips. If a commission
      hasn't been marked as paid 30 days after the travel date, it moves to the "Chasing List"
      and drafts a follow-up email to the supplier.

2. Smart Reconciliation (The "Bank Sync")
   ●​ The Process: Upload a CSV or PDF of your bank statement or an EFT (Electronic
      Funds Transfer) from a supplier (e.g., a Marriott commission statement).
   ●​ AI Task: Kimi k2.5 reads the statement, identifies the PNR or Client Name, and
      matches it to the Itinerary_ID.
   ●​ Result: Instead of manual entry, the agent just clicks "Confirm" on the matches Kimi
      found.



III. Module 2: Invoicing (Service Fees & Payments)
Goal: Professional, tax-compliant billing for the client.

1. Service Fee Management
  ●​ Types: Flat Fee (e.g., $50 USD per person), Percentage (e.g., 10% of total), or
     "Consulting Retainer."
  ●​ Logic: Service fees are usually 100% profit. The CRM must track these separately from
     "Supplier Payments" to give an accurate view of the agency's pure margin.

2. The Payment Portal
  ●​ Fields: Payment_Method (Transfer, Credit Card, Cash), Transaction_ID, Deposit_Date.
  ●​ Client View: The "Interactive Link" (from the Itinerary Builder) includes a "Pay Now"
     section showing:
         ○​ Total Trip Cost.
         ○​ Amount Paid to Date.
         ○​ Remaining Balance + Due Date.

V. Developer's Database Schema (Financials)
Table: financial_transactions
  ●​ id, itinerary_id (FK), type [Supplier_Payment, Client_Receipt, Service_Fee]
  ●​ amount_original, currency_original, exchange_rate
  ●​ amount_mxn (Standardized for reporting)
  ●​ status [Pending, Cleared, Cancelled]

Table: commissions
  ●​ id, itinerary_id (FK), supplier_id (FK)
  ●​ expected_amount, actual_received, received_date
  ●​ commission_rate (Pulls from Supplier Profile)



VI. AI Financial Insights (Owner View)
  ●​ "Profit Leakage" Alert: Kimi flags if an agent forgot to add a service fee or if a
     supplier's commission is lower than the agency's negotiated rate.
  ●​ Cash Flow Forecast: Kimi predicts the next 90 days of revenue based on "Final
     Payment" due dates from active itineraries.
